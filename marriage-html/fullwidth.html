Altitude Tips: Mammoth is at 8,000 ft elevation!

Mammoth Lakes, CA is located at 8,900 ft above sea level. Our venue, McCoy Station, is even higher at 9,630 feet. Altitude sickness is real, but can be avoided. To ensure you have a great time, please read the below acclimation tips!


1. Arrive Early - getting acclimated to higher elevations than you’re used to can take anywhere from 1-3 days at a given altitude. Try to take it easy for the first 24 hours of your trip.


2. Watch What You Eat…and Drink —when all is said and done, complex carbohydrates allow you to use oxygen more efficiently and help maintain your energy levels—both important factors in preventing altitude sickness. So leave your Atkins Diet book at home, and plan on eating plenty of complex carbohydrates including whole grains, fruits, vegetables and legumes. The effects of caffeine and alcohol are also exacerbated at altitude, so be conscious of your intake throughout the weekend.


3. Water. Water. Water. – You cannot have enough. Start drinking during your drive up, and keep drinking throughout your stay in Mammoth. Electrolyte rich beverages, like Gatorade, are also great to have on hand.


4. Medications – If you decide you could be at risk for altitude sickness, book an appointment with your doctor or travel medicine specialist. If feasible, they may recommend acetazolamide, often marketed under the brand name Diamox, which is the most tried and tested drug for altitude sickness prevention and treatment. We also advise you to pack ibuprofen (effective for relieving altitude-induced headaches) and ginger chews, capsules or tea, which can help quell altitude-induced stomach nausea.


If you do begin to feel the symptoms of altitude sickness, you may consider using an oxygen canister. You can pre purchase these on Amazon or at big box retailers like Target. John and Sarah will have oxygen available at the wedding…just in case.


5. Know the Danger Signs: consider descending to a lower altitude if you experience moderate to severe levels of these symptoms:

  • Headaches
  • Dizziness
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • Fatigue/loss of energy
  • Insomnia
