Transportation in Mammoth Local Shuttles & More

No Car? No Problem!

Bus: Mammoth has great public transportation that connects most of the town to our event spaces. Buses pick up every 15 minutes at most stops, so they're a really convenient way to get around town. See maps and more information here.


Shuttle: Shuttles to and from the wedding will be provided from the village.


Taxi: Mammoth Taxi – 760-934-8294

Driving to Mammoth

From Los Angeles

Driving from Southern California will take approximately 6 hours.


Take the 405 freeway north to the 5 freeway north to Highway 14 north to Highway 395 north. Exit on Highway 203 to Mammoth Lakes. Highway 203 turns into Old Mammoth Road and leads you to the center of town.


From San Diego / Orange County

Driving from the San Diego area will take approximately 7 hours and from the Orange County area will take approximately 6 hours.


Take the 15 freeway north to Highway 395 north. Exit on Highway 203 to Mammoth Lakes. Highway 203 turns into Old Mammoth Road and leads you to the center of town.


From San Francisco / Bay Area

Driving from the San Francisco / Sacramento areas will take approximately 7-8 hours


Depending on road conditions, various routes may be taken, but in the winter – John and Sarah recommend taking the 50 east to the 89 south to the 395 south. Exit on Highway 203 to Mammoth Lakes. Please consult Caltrans at 800-427-7623 to assess road closures through the Yosemite/Tahoe area.


For guests coming from San Francisco, we highly recommend taking the Thursday night shuttle to avoid potential winter weather through Tahoe.



No matter where you’re coming from, drive safely please!


Flying to Mammoth Airport Code: MMH

Guests can fly nonstop to Mammoth Lakes (MMH) from Los Angeles, San Francisco San Diego, and Denver.


From Mammoth Yosemite Airport (MMH), you are only a 10 minute drive to Mammoth Lakes. Please see visitmammoth.com or check with your hotel for information about Airport Shuttles available to you.


If you do decide to fly, we recommend that you leave ample time to arrive before the wedding and that you arrange a contingency plan for poor weather. Flights are often canceled in the winter, and we would hate for you to miss a powder day!

Driving from San Francisco, in particular, involves driving through winding mountain passes that are not an issue if you are coming from the south.
